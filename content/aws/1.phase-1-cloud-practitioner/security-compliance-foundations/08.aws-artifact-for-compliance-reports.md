# AWS Artifact for Compliance Reports

When an auditor asks "prove your cloud provider is secure" or a regulation requires you to have a signed agreement with your cloud vendor, you need **AWS Artifact**. It is your one-stop portal for compliance documents and agreements -- available for free inside your AWS console.

Think of AWS Artifact as a filing cabinet full of security certificates and legal agreements. Whenever someone asks for proof, you open the cabinet, grab the right document, and hand it over.

### Artifact Reports

This section contains **compliance documents from independent auditors** that verify AWS meets specific security standards. You can download and share these documents with your own auditors, regulators, or customers.

**Available documents include:**

| Document | What It Proves |
|----------|---------------|
| **SOC 1 Report** | AWS controls related to financial reporting are effective |
| **SOC 2 Report** | AWS security, availability, and confidentiality controls are effective |
| **SOC 3 Report** | Public summary of SOC 2 findings (shareable without NDA) |
| **ISO 27001 Certificate** | AWS follows international information security management standards |
| **PCI DSS AOC** | AWS infrastructure meets payment card data security requirements |
| **FedRAMP Package** | AWS meets US federal government security standards |

Some documents (like SOC 2) require you to accept a **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)** before downloading, because they contain detailed security information about AWS infrastructure.

### Artifact Agreements

This section lets you **review and accept legal agreements** between your company and AWS. These agreements are required for specific compliance programs.

| Agreement | Required For | What It Does |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **BAA** (Business Associate Agreement) | HIPAA (healthcare data) | AWS agrees to handle Protected Health Information according to HIPAA rules |
| **DPA** (Data Processing Addendum) | GDPR (European data) | Defines how AWS processes personal data under EU privacy laws |
| **Australia Privacy Act** | Australian data regulations | AWS commits to Australian privacy requirements |

**Important:** You can accept agreements for a single account or for your entire organization (through AWS Organizations). Organization-level agreements apply to all member accounts automatically.

1. **Navigate to AWS Artifact** in the AWS Management Console (search for "Artifact")
2. Choose **Reports** for compliance documents or **Agreements** for legal documents
3. **Browse or search** for the specific document you need
4. **Accept the NDA** (if required) and **download** the document
5. Share with your auditor, compliance team, or customer

**Access control:** Use IAM policies to control who in your organization can access Artifact. Not everyone needs access to sensitive compliance reports.

## Common Use Cases

| Who | What They Need | Artifact Section |
|-----|---------------|-----------------|
| **External auditor** | SOC 2 report to verify AWS security controls | Reports |
| **Compliance officer** | ISO 27001 certificate for annual compliance review | Reports |
| **Legal team** | BAA to sign before deploying healthcare workloads | Agreements |
| **Privacy team** | GDPR Data Processing Addendum for EU operations | Agreements |
| **Sales team** | SOC 3 summary to share with potential customers | Reports |
| **Security team** | PCI DSS attestation for payment processing compliance | Reports |

## Artifact Is Free and Always Available

AWS Artifact costs nothing to use. Every AWS account has access to it. You do not need to contact AWS Support or wait for documents to be mailed. The portal is always available with the latest versions of all compliance documents.

## Key Takeaways

- **AWS Artifact** is a free, self-service portal for compliance documents and agreements
- **Artifact Reports** provides downloadable audit reports (SOC, ISO, PCI, FedRAMP) from independent auditors
- **Artifact Agreements** lets you review and accept legal documents like the HIPAA BAA and GDPR DPA
- Some reports require accepting an **NDA** before download because they contain sensitive security details
- Use Artifact when auditors or regulators need proof that AWS meets specific compliance standards
- Agreements can be accepted for a **single account** or an **entire organization** through AWS Organizations
