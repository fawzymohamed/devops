# Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

A password alone is not enough to protect your AWS account. Passwords get stolen through phishing, data breaches, and social engineering. **Multi-factor authentication (MFA)** adds a second layer of defense so that even a stolen password cannot unlock your account.

Think of MFA like a bank vault that needs both a key and a combination code. Having just one does not open the door.

## What Is MFA?

MFA requires **two different types of proof** to verify your identity:

1. **Something you know** -- your password
2. **Something you have** -- a physical or virtual device that generates a one-time code

When you log in with MFA enabled, you enter your password first. Then AWS asks for a **time-based one-time password (TOTP)** from your MFA device. This code changes every 30 seconds, so stealing it is nearly useless.

## Types of MFA Devices

AWS supports three types of MFA devices:

| MFA Type | What It Is | Cost | Best For |
|----------|-----------|------|----------|
| **Virtual MFA** | App on your phone (Google Authenticator, Authy, Microsoft Authenticator) | Free | Most users -- easy to set up |
| **Hardware TOTP token** | Physical key fob that displays a code (Gemalto) | ~$13-20 | Users who prefer a dedicated device |
| **FIDO2 security key** | USB/NFC device (YubiKey, Titan Security Key) | ~$25-50 | Highest security -- phishing resistant |

### Virtual MFA (Most Common)

A smartphone app generates a new 6-digit code every 30 seconds. You scan a QR code during setup, and the app starts producing codes. Popular apps include **Google Authenticator**, **Authy**, and **Microsoft Authenticator**.

**Pros:** Free, convenient, works on any smartphone
**Cons:** If you lose your phone, you lose access (back up your recovery codes)

### Hardware TOTP Token

A small physical device with a screen that shows a 6-digit code. It works the same as a virtual MFA app but does not need a smartphone or battery charging.

**Pros:** Dedicated device, harder to compromise remotely
**Cons:** Costs money, can be lost or damaged

### FIDO2 Security Key

A USB or NFC device you plug into your computer or tap against your phone. Instead of typing a code, you press a button on the key. FIDO2 keys are **phishing-resistant** because they verify both the website and the user.

**Pros:** Most secure option, resistant to phishing attacks
**Cons:** Most expensive, requires carrying a physical device

The root account can delete all resources, close the account, and change billing information. If an attacker gets root access without MFA, they can lock you out completely. **Enabling MFA on root should be the first thing you do with a new AWS account.**

## Best Practices for MFA

- **Enable MFA on every IAM user**, not just the root account
- **Use a dedicated MFA device** for the root account (separate from your personal phone)
- **Store backup codes** in a secure location in case you lose your device
- **Use IAM policies** to require MFA for sensitive operations (like deleting resources)
- **Consider FIDO2 keys** for high-security accounts where phishing is a concern

## Key Takeaways

- MFA combines something you know (password) with something you have (device) for stronger security
- AWS supports **virtual MFA apps**, **hardware TOTP tokens**, and **FIDO2 security keys**
- **FIDO2 security keys** are the most secure option because they are phishing-resistant
- **Root account MFA is non-negotiable** -- enable it immediately on every AWS account
- MFA protects you even when passwords are compromised -- the attacker still needs your device
- Virtual MFA apps like Google Authenticator are free and the easiest way to get started
