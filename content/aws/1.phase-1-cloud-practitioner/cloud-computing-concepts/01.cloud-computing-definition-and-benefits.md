# Cloud Computing Definition & Benefits

**Cloud computing** is the on-demand delivery of IT resources over the internet with pay-as-you-go pricing. Instead of buying and maintaining physical data centers and servers, you rent compute power, storage, and databases from a cloud provider like AWS.

## What Makes It "Cloud"?

Three things define cloud computing:

- **On-demand** -- you get resources when you need them, not weeks later
- **Over the internet** -- you access everything through the network, not local hardware
- **Pay-as-you-go** -- you pay only for what you use, like a utility bill

Think of it like electricity. You do not build a power plant to light your house. You plug into the grid and pay for what you consume. Cloud computing works the same way for IT resources.

## Cloud vs. Traditional IT

| Aspect | Traditional IT | Cloud Computing |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Hardware** | You buy and own servers | The cloud provider owns servers |
| **Setup time** | Weeks or months | Minutes |
| **Upfront cost** | Large capital expense | No upfront cost |
| **Scaling** | Buy more hardware manually | Scale up or down automatically |
| **Maintenance** | Your team patches and repairs | Provider handles infrastructure |
| **Capacity planning** | Guess future needs | Adjust in real time |

With traditional IT, you estimate how much hardware you need, purchase it, wait for delivery, install it, and hope your guess was right. Cloud computing removes this guesswork.

## Six Benefits of Cloud Computing

AWS highlights six core benefits that drive organizations to adopt cloud computing.

### 1. Trade Upfront Expense for Variable Expense

Instead of investing heavily in data centers before you know how you will use them, you pay only when you consume resources. No large upfront purchases.

### 2. Benefit from Massive Economies of Scale

Cloud providers serve hundreds of thousands of customers. This aggregate usage lets them achieve **lower pay-as-you-go prices** than you could get on your own.

### 3. Stop Guessing Capacity

You no longer need to predict how much infrastructure you will need. If you need more, scale up. If you need less, scale down. You pay only for what you actually use.

### 4. Increase Speed and Agility

New IT resources are a click away. Instead of weeks to provision a server, you can launch one in **minutes**. This lets your team experiment and innovate faster.

### 5. Stop Spending Money Running Data Centers

Managing physical infrastructure -- power, cooling, racking servers, security guards -- takes time and money. Cloud computing lets your team focus on building products instead of maintaining hardware.

### 6. Go Global in Minutes

Deploy your application to **multiple AWS Regions** around the world with just a few clicks. Your users get low latency because the application runs closer to them.

## Key Takeaways

- Cloud computing is on-demand IT resources over the internet with pay-as-you-go pricing
- You rent infrastructure instead of buying it, which removes large upfront costs
- Six key benefits: variable expense, economies of scale, right-sized capacity, speed, no data center management, and global reach
- Cloud computing lets you scale resources up or down in minutes instead of weeks
