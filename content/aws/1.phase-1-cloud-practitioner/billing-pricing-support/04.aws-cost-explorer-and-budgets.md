# AWS Cost Explorer & Budgets

After you deploy workloads on AWS, you need to track what you are actually spending. **AWS Cost Explorer** analyzes your past and current costs. **AWS Budgets** alerts you when spending approaches or exceeds limits you set.

## AWS Cost Explorer

Cost Explorer is a visual tool in the AWS Billing console that shows your **actual AWS spending** over time. It updates at least daily and stores up to 12 months of historical data.

### What You Can Do

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Visualize trends** | See spending by day, month, or over the past year as line or bar charts |
| **Filter and group** | Break down costs by service, account, Region, tag, or instance type |
| **Forecast** | Predict future spending (up to 12 months ahead) based on usage patterns |
| **Custom reports** | Save and share reports tailored to your team's needs |
| **Identify anomalies** | Spot unexpected cost spikes and drill into the cause |

### Filtering and Grouping

Cost Explorer lets you slice your data in many ways:

- **By service** -- how much you spent on EC2 vs. S3 vs. RDS
- **By linked account** -- which AWS account in your organization spent the most
- **By Region** -- compare spending across us-east-1, eu-west-1, etc.
- **By tag** -- view costs by project, team, or environment (requires cost allocation tags)
- **By instance type** -- identify which EC2 instance types drive the most cost

**Example:** You notice your total bill jumped last month. You filter by service and discover that EC2 costs doubled. You then filter by instance type and find that someone launched several large instances in a non-production account.

### Forecasting

Cost Explorer uses your historical usage to **predict future costs**. This helps you:

- Estimate next month's bill before it arrives
- Plan budgets for upcoming quarters
- Spot trends that suggest costs will grow

Forecasts become more accurate as your usage history grows.

### Right Sizing Recommendations

Cost Explorer includes **right sizing recommendations** that analyze your EC2 usage and suggest smaller instance types where you are paying for more capacity than you need. This can save money without affecting performance.

## AWS Budgets

AWS Budgets lets you set a **spending threshold** and get notified when your costs approach or exceed it.

### Budget Types

| Budget Type | What It Tracks |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Cost budget** | Total dollar spending (most common) |
| **Usage budget** | Service usage (e.g., EC2 hours, S3 GB) |
| **Reservation budget** | Reserved Instance and Savings Plan utilization |
| **Savings Plans budget** | Savings Plan coverage percentage |

### How Budgets Work

1. **Set a budget amount** -- for example, $500 per month
2. **Configure alert thresholds** -- get notified at 80%, 100%, or any percentage you choose
3. **Choose notification methods** -- email addresses and/or Amazon SNS topics
4. **Optional: set automated actions** -- AWS can apply an IAM policy to restrict spending or stop specific resources when a threshold is crossed

**Example:** You create a $200/month budget for your development account. You set alerts at 50% ($100), 80% ($160), and 100% ($200). When spending hits $100, you get an email. At $160, you get another. At $200, an automated action restricts the account from launching new EC2 instances.

### Zero-Spend Budget

A **zero-spend budget** alerts you at the first dollar of charges. This is especially useful for Free Tier accounts where you expect to pay nothing.

## Cost and Usage Report (CUR)

For the most detailed billing data, use the **AWS Cost and Usage Report**. It provides line-item data for every charge, including resource IDs. CUR delivers files to an S3 bucket where you can analyze them with Athena, QuickSight, or your own tools.

| Tool | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Cost Explorer** | Visual analysis, trends, quick answers |
| **AWS Budgets** | Setting alerts and automated spending controls |
| **Cost and Usage Report** | Detailed accounting, custom analysis, integration with BI tools |

## Key Takeaways

- **Cost Explorer** visualizes your actual AWS spending with filters, grouping, and up to 12 months of history
- Filter by service, account, Region, tag, or instance type to find cost drivers
- Cost Explorer **forecasts** future spending based on historical patterns
- **AWS Budgets** sends alerts when spending approaches or exceeds thresholds you define
- Budget alerts can trigger **automated actions** like restricting IAM permissions
- Create a **zero-spend budget** to catch unexpected charges on Free Tier accounts
- Use the **Cost and Usage Report** for detailed line-item billing data for accounting
