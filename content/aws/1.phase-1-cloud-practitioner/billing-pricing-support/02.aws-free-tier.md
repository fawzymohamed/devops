# AWS Free Tier

The **AWS Free Tier** lets you explore and experiment with AWS services at no cost. It is available to all new AWS accounts and includes some offers that never expire. Understanding the Free Tier categories and their limits helps you learn AWS without unexpected bills.

### Always Free

These offers **never expire** and apply to all AWS accounts, not just new ones.

| Service | Free Allowance |
|---------|---------------|
| **AWS Lambda** | 1 million requests + 400,000 GB-seconds of compute per month |
| **Amazon DynamoDB** | 25 GB of storage + 25 read/write capacity units |
| **Amazon SNS** | 1 million publishes per month |
| **Amazon SQS** | 1 million requests per month |
| **Amazon CloudWatch** | 10 custom metrics + 10 alarms |
| **AWS CloudTrail** | 1 management trail (90-day event history) |

### 12 Months Free

Available for the **first 12 months** after you create your AWS account.

| Service | Free Allowance |
|---------|---------------|
| **Amazon EC2** | 750 hours per month (t2.micro or t3.micro) |
| **Amazon S3** | 5 GB of standard storage |
| **Amazon RDS** | 750 hours per month (db.t2.micro or db.t3.micro) |
| **Amazon CloudFront** | 1 TB of data transfer out per month |
| **Elastic Load Balancing** | 750 hours per month |

**Important:** The 12-month clock starts on the day you create your account. If you sign up in January but do not use EC2 until July, you only have 6 months of free EC2 remaining.

### Trials

Short-term free trials for specific services. The duration varies by service.

| Service | Trial Details |
|---------|--------------|
| **Amazon SageMaker** | 2 months of selected free tier usage |
| **Amazon Redshift** | 2-month trial with 750 hours per month |
| **Amazon Inspector** | 15-day free trial |
| **Amazon GuardDuty** | 30-day free trial |

## What Happens When You Exceed Limits

AWS does **not** automatically stop your resources when you pass Free Tier limits. You start paying **standard On-Demand rates** for any usage above the free allowance.

**Example:** The EC2 Free Tier gives you 750 hours per month on a t3.micro. If you run **two** t3.micro instances 24/7, that equals ~1,440 hours. You pay On-Demand rates for the 690 hours above 750.

### Common Surprise Charges

| Mistake | Why It Costs Money |
|---------|-------------------|
| Forgetting to stop EC2 instances | Instance hours accumulate even if you are not using them |
| Using a larger instance type | Free Tier only covers t2.micro or t3.micro |
| Leaving EBS volumes attached | Storage charges apply even if the instance is stopped |
| Data transfer out | Exceeding the monthly free allowance |
| Running in multiple Regions | Free Tier limits are **per account**, not per Region |

## Avoiding Unexpected Bills

1. **Set up a billing alarm** -- In AWS Budgets, create a zero-spend budget that alerts you at the first dollar
2. **Check the Free Tier dashboard** -- The AWS Billing console shows your Free Tier usage and how close you are to limits
3. **Stop unused resources** -- Terminate or stop EC2 instances, delete unused EBS volumes, and empty S3 buckets you do not need
4. **Use the right instance size** -- Only t2.micro and t3.micro qualify for the EC2 Free Tier
5. **Monitor with AWS Budgets** -- Set up email alerts for any cost threshold you choose

## Key Takeaways

- The AWS Free Tier has three categories: **Always Free**, **12 Months Free**, and **Trials**
- Always Free offers (Lambda, DynamoDB, SNS) never expire and apply to all accounts
- The 12-month period starts when you **create your account**, not when you first use a service
- When you exceed Free Tier limits, you pay standard On-Demand rates -- AWS does not stop your resources
- Set up **billing alerts** in AWS Budgets to avoid surprise charges
- Always terminate resources you are not using, especially EC2 instances and EBS volumes
