# CLF-C02 Exam Format & Strategy

The **AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02)** is the foundational AWS certification. It validates your understanding of AWS Cloud concepts, services, security, and pricing. Knowing the exam format and having a clear strategy gives you a real advantage on test day.

## Exam Structure at a Glance

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Exam code** | CLF-C02 |
| **Questions** | 65 total (50 scored + 15 unscored) |
| **Time limit** | 90 minutes |
| **Passing score** | 700 out of 1000 (scaled) |
| **Format** | Multiple choice and multiple response |
| **Cost** | $100 USD |
| **Delivery** | Pearson VUE testing center or online proctored |

You will not know which 15 questions are unscored. AWS uses them to evaluate questions for future exams. Treat every question as if it counts.

## Question Types

**Multiple choice** -- one correct answer out of four options. Most questions are this type.

**Multiple response** -- two or more correct answers out of five or six options. The question tells you exactly how many to select (for example, "Select TWO").

There are no fill-in-the-blank, drag-and-drop, or free-response questions.

### Domain Breakdown

| Domain | Weight | Key Topics |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Cloud Concepts** | 24% | Cloud benefits, Well-Architected Framework, cloud economics, deployment models |
| **Security & Compliance** | 30% | Shared Responsibility Model, IAM, encryption, compliance programs, security services |
| **Cloud Technology & Services** | 34% | EC2, S3, RDS, Lambda, VPC, CloudFront, Route 53, compute/storage/database/networking categories |
| **Billing, Pricing & Support** | 12% | Pricing models, Free Tier, Cost Explorer, Budgets, support plans, Trusted Advisor |

**Strategy:** Technology & Services and Security together cover **64% of the exam**. Master these two domains first.

## Time Management

You have **90 minutes for 65 questions** -- that is about **1 minute 23 seconds per question**.

- **First pass (60 minutes):** Answer every question. Flag ones you are unsure about. Do not get stuck on any single question.
- **Second pass (20 minutes):** Return to flagged questions. Reread them with fresh eyes.
- **Final check (10 minutes):** Verify you answered every question. Review any remaining flags.

Never leave a question blank. There is **no penalty for guessing**. Eliminate wrong answers and pick the best remaining option.

## Elimination Strategy

Most questions have one or two obviously wrong options. Eliminate those first.

1. **Read the question carefully** -- watch for words like "MOST," "LEAST," "NOT," and "BEST"
2. **Eliminate absurd options** -- answers that contradict basic cloud concepts
3. **Watch for AWS-specific traps** -- "customer responsibility" vs. "AWS responsibility" questions
4. **Pick the most complete answer** -- if two options seem correct, choose the one that covers more ground

## Study Plan Tips

| Time Before Exam | Focus Area |
|-----------------|------------|
| **4 weeks out** | Cloud Concepts + Security fundamentals |
| **3 weeks out** | Core AWS services (EC2, S3, RDS, Lambda, VPC) |
| **2 weeks out** | Additional services + Billing & Support |
| **1 week out** | Practice exams + review weak areas |
| **Day before** | Light review, rest well, no cramming |

## Exam Day Checklist

- **Arrive early** -- 15 minutes for testing centers, 30 minutes for online proctoring setup
- **Bring valid ID** -- two forms of identification for testing centers
- **Read every question twice** -- exam questions are worded carefully
- **Watch for qualifier words** -- "MOST cost-effective," "LEAST operational overhead," "BEST practice"
- **Use the flag feature** -- mark questions to review later
- **Stay calm** -- if you studied the material, you know more than you think

## Common Pitfalls

- **Overthinking simple questions** -- the Cloud Practitioner exam tests breadth, not depth
- **Confusing similar services** -- know the difference between Security Groups and NACLs, S3 and EBS, RDS and DynamoDB
- **Ignoring the Shared Responsibility Model** -- this appears in many questions across all domains
- **Not reading all options** -- the first "right-sounding" answer may not be the best answer
- **Poor time management** -- spending 5 minutes on one question means rushing through five others

## Key Takeaways

- The CLF-C02 has 65 questions in 90 minutes, with a passing score of 700/1000
- Four domains: Cloud Concepts (24%), Security (30%), Technology (34%), and Billing (12%)
- No penalty for guessing -- always answer every question
- Use a two-pass approach: answer everything first, then review flagged questions
- Focus study time on Technology & Services and Security -- they make up 64% of the exam
