# Practice Questions: Billing & Support

This domain covers **12% of the CLF-C02 exam** -- the lightest domain, but still worth about 8 scored questions. Questions test pricing models, billing tools, support plans, and cost optimization. Below are 10 realistic practice questions.

## Tips for Billing Questions

- **Know the four pricing models** and when each is best (On-Demand, Reserved, Spot, Savings Plans)
- **Distinguish between billing tools** -- Cost Explorer (visualize), Budgets (alert), Pricing Calculator (estimate)
- **Support plan tiers** are commonly tested -- know what each tier includes

---

## Practice Questions

### Question 1

**A startup is testing a new application and needs compute resources for an unpredictable amount of time. They do not want any long-term commitment. Which pricing model is BEST?**

- A) Reserved Instances
- B) On-Demand Instances
- C) Spot Instances
- D) Savings Plans

> **Answer: B** -- On-Demand Instances have no upfront cost and no long-term commitment. You pay by the second or hour and can stop anytime. This is ideal for testing, prototyping, and unpredictable workloads where you cannot estimate usage.

---

### Question 2

**Which AWS tool helps you estimate the monthly cost of a new architecture BEFORE you deploy it?**

- A) AWS Cost Explorer
- B) AWS Budgets
- C) AWS Pricing Calculator
- D) AWS Cost and Usage Report

> **Answer: C** -- The AWS Pricing Calculator lets you model your architecture and estimate costs before you spend anything. Cost Explorer shows past spending. Budgets alert on thresholds. The Cost and Usage Report provides detailed historical billing data.

---

### Question 3

**What is the primary benefit of AWS consolidated billing?**

- A) It gives each account a separate bill for auditing
- B) It combines usage across accounts for potential volume discounts and a single bill
- C) It eliminates all AWS charges
- D) It automatically chooses the cheapest pricing model

> **Answer: B** -- Consolidated billing combines usage from all accounts in an organization into one bill. The combined usage may qualify for volume discounts (for example, tiered S3 or data transfer pricing). It simplifies billing management without eliminating charges.

---

### Question 4

**Which AWS Support plan provides access to a Technical Account Manager (TAM)?**

- A) Basic Support
- B) Developer Support
- C) Business Support
- D) Enterprise Support

> **Answer: D** -- Only Enterprise Support includes a designated Technical Account Manager (TAM). The TAM provides proactive guidance, architectural reviews, and helps optimize your AWS environment. This is the most expensive plan but includes the most comprehensive support.

---

### Question 5

**A company wants to see a breakdown of which departments are spending the most on AWS. What feature helps them track costs by department?**

- A) Security groups
- B) Cost allocation tags
- C) IAM policies
- D) VPC flow logs

> **Answer: B** -- Cost allocation tags let you label AWS resources with metadata like "Department: Marketing" or "Project: WebApp." After activating these tags in the billing console, you can filter and group costs by tag in Cost Explorer and the Cost and Usage Report.

---

### Question 6

**Which of the following are included in the AWS Free Tier? (Select TWO)**

- A) 750 hours per month of EC2 t2.micro or t3.micro for 12 months
- B) Unlimited S3 storage forever
- C) 5 GB of S3 Standard storage for 12 months
- D) Free Enterprise Support for the first year
- E) Unlimited Lambda invocations forever

> **Answer: A, C** -- The Free Tier includes 750 hours/month of eligible EC2 instances and 5 GB of S3 Standard storage, both for 12 months. S3 storage is not unlimited. Enterprise Support is never free. Lambda offers 1 million free requests per month (always free), but not unlimited.

---

### Question 7

**AWS Trusted Advisor provides recommendations in which areas? (Select TWO)**

- A) Cost optimization
- B) Application code quality
- C) Security
- D) Database schema design

> **Answer: A, C** -- Trusted Advisor checks five categories: cost optimization, security, fault tolerance, performance, and service limits. It does not review application code quality (that is CodeGuru) or database schema design.

---

### Question 8

**A research team runs batch processing jobs that can tolerate interruption. Which pricing model should they choose for the BIGGEST cost savings?**

- A) On-Demand Instances
- B) Reserved Instances
- C) Spot Instances
- D) Savings Plans

> **Answer: C** -- Spot Instances offer up to 90% savings compared to On-Demand. The tradeoff is that AWS can reclaim them with a 2-minute warning. Batch processing, data analysis, and rendering are ideal for Spot because these workloads can be interrupted and restarted without data loss.

---

### Question 9

**What is the response time for a business-critical system down issue on the AWS Business Support plan?**

- A) 15 minutes
- B) Under 1 hour
- C) Under 4 hours
- D) Under 24 hours

> **Answer: B** -- AWS Business Support offers a response time of less than 1 hour for production system down (urgent) cases. Enterprise Support offers a 15-minute response for business-critical system down. Developer Support does not have a production-down response tier.

---

### Question 10

**A company wants to compare the cost of running servers on-premises versus on AWS. What is this comparison called?**

- A) Return on Investment (ROI)
- B) Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) analysis
- C) Cost allocation
- D) Reserved Instance planning

> **Answer: B** -- A Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) analysis compares the full cost of on-premises infrastructure (hardware, power, cooling, staff, maintenance) with AWS cloud costs. This helps organizations build a business case for cloud migration by showing potential savings.

## Key Takeaways

- Know the four pricing models cold: On-Demand (flexibility), Reserved (steady-state savings), Spot (biggest savings, interruptible), Savings Plans (flexible commitment)
- Three billing tools: Cost Explorer (visualize past costs), Budgets (set alerts), Pricing Calculator (estimate future costs)
- Support plans from cheapest to most comprehensive: Basic, Developer, Business, Enterprise
- Only Enterprise Support includes a TAM (Technical Account Manager)
- Consolidated billing combines usage for volume discounts, not to eliminate charges
- Data transfer into AWS is free; data transfer out is charged
